01 · The Problem

No unified view of seasonal performance across EU markets

The Merch EU team had no centralised system to track how seasonal events and cultural themes were driving product sales across five distinct marketplaces. Data lived in raw SQL tables, decisions were reactive, and year-on-year comparisons were manual and error-prone.

[01]

Fragmented data

Order data existed in raw database tables (orcadb.orca_ddl.merch_orders) with no thematic categorisation. Identifying Christmas vs Halloween vs Oktoberfest performance required custom one-off SQL queries each time.

[02]

No year-on-year benchmarking

The team could not quickly compare a theme's performance against the prior year. Without this, promotional planning, budget allocation, and forecasting were based on intuition rather than data.

[03]

EU ≠ US — market-specific gaps

Existing theme frameworks were US-centric. EU events like Karneval, UK Coronation, and Bastille Day were untracked. The solution had to be purpose-built for EU5, with multilingual keyword mapping across UK, DE, FR, IT, and SP.

03 · Theme Taxonomy

49 themes across 3 strategic categories

Themes were classified into three distinct types based on their commercial nature and seasonality profile — enabling different analytical and forecasting approaches for each.

ANNUAL EVENTS

Periodic & Predictable

Events that recur each year. Tracked as growth opportunities with year-on-year comparison. Promotional planning and advertising budgets are aligned to these peaks.

CONSTANT THEMES

Year-Round Baseline

Themes prevalent throughout the full calendar cycle with little seasonal variation. These form the baseline of total sales performance and are critical for forecasting.

ONE-OFF THEMES

Unique & High-Volume

Events unlikely to recur but generating significant sales volume — such as the UK Coronation or Euro Cup. Captured separately to avoid distorting baseline trends.

05 · Dashboard Design

Two-section QuickSight architecture

The dashboard was structured into two purpose-built sections — one for in-week operational decisions and one for strategic annual review — each with distinct filter logic and data sources.

§1

Current Week Theme

Displays the current week's performance metrics in the Merch WBR (Weekly Business Review) format. Designed for operational decision-making and real-time campaign monitoring.

  • 5 embedded tables per view
  • WMQ toggle (Week / Month / Quarter)
  • Marketplace filter (UK, DE, FR, IT, SP)
  • Theme filter with 49 categories
  • Product type and Brand filters
  • 2024 EU Theme % (Units) — % share view
  • Best Seller ASIN per theme (image + title)
  • Day of month filter for operations sheet
§2

Summary of the Year

Strategic annual view comparing current year against 3 prior years. Used for seasonal planning, budget setting, and identifying emerging vs. declining themes.

  • 3-year unit sales comparison by theme
  • Past Year filter for historical drilling
  • 2023 Theme % by Units (EU) — weekly stack chart
  • 2023 Theme % by Units (US) — reference benchmark
  • Stacked bar chart — 52 weeks, all themes
  • Rolling trend line by theme category
  • Annual event schedule with revenue actuals
  • Year-on-year increment column (LY vs TY)
