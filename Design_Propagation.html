01 · The Opportunity

Why propagate top t‑shirt designs to winter categories?

In Aug’24, EU Merch observed that designs propagated from t‑shirts to Winter Categories (hoodie, sweatshirt, long‑sleeve) performed +26% better than non‑propagated designs. Only 3.2K propagated designs already contributed to 55% of Winter Category OPS and 34% of AWAS. Yet the vast majority of top‑selling t‑shirt designs (those driving 80% of t‑shirt sales) had never been propagated. This represented a massive untapped revenue opportunity.

The algorithm takes into account each marketplace individually, ensuring that only designs with proven local demand (based on sales velocity) are propagated. For example, a design that sells well only in Germany will be propagated to DE winter categories, while a pan‑EU design will be expanded to all five markets.

04 · Extension to Amazon Music

Artist‑based cross‑propagation using listening preferences

The same propagation logic was extended to the Amazon Music team. By analysing top‑performing artists’ designs from the global Amazon Music platform, we identified designs with high customer affinity (based on streaming data, playlist inclusion, and search trends). These designs were then cross‑propagated into EU Merch winter categories, ensuring that localised artist merch became available across the EU in time for peak seasons.

05 · Outcomes & Future Standardisation

A new standard practice for seasonal category acceleration

The Winter Selection Propagation project demonstrated that systematically migrating top t‑shirt designs to higher‑margin winter categories is a repeatable, high‑ROI mechanism. After presenting the analysis to leadership, the propagation logic was approved for full automation, and the process is now being rolled out as a standard Q3‑Q4 practice for all EU seasonal categories. The extension to Amazon Music proved the model’s versatility, opening doors for future collaborations with Prime Video, Audible, and other Amazon entertainment properties.
