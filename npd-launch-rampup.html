03 · Inventory Strategy

10,000-unit allocation across 2 variants

Inventory planning was critical to avoid both stockouts and overstock. Based on historical mascara category data and COTY's retail sales forecast, we allocated 70% of launch inventory to Variant 002 (Black) and 30% to Variant 001 (Brown), reflecting typical category performance ratios.

Supply Chain Mechanics

  • → "NP" Order Types: Utilized "Not yet published" (NP) Order Type codes via CMBS to force PO generation while keeping ASINs hidden from the front-end.
  • → Exception Coding: Leveraged Reason Code MB063 (New country launch) and MB075 to bypass standard forecast overrides.
  • → Pan-EU Distribution: Directed initial Purchase Orders across key nodes (e.g., HAM2 in DE, XMA3 in IT) to ensure Prime eligibility on Day 1.

Retail Marketing Synergy

  • → Storefront Billboards: Activated 1300x90px Hero Billboards on EU Beauty Homepage linked directly to the Rimmel brand store, synchronized with the Oct 1 Media push.
  • → Subscribe & Save (SnS): Immediately enrolled Megalift ASINs into SnS with a 5% vendor-funded discount. Based on historical category data, SnS funding drives an average incremental revenue lift of >30%.
  • → Retention Funnel: Capitalized on the statistic that SnS coupons generate a 19x ROI to lock in early adopters.
04 · Execution Challenges

Navigating complexity in cross-functional launch coordination

CHALLENGES ENCOUNTERED

Key Obstacles

Tight timeline — 8 weeks from briefing to launch with holiday season in between
ASIN creation delays due to image compliance requirements (lifestyle shots needed reshoot)
Brand video file size exceeded Amazon limits (had to re-encode and optimize)
Vendor Central forecast accuracy concerns — COTY initially proposed 15K units (rejected due to category velocity data)
Shipment window coordination — needed to align with COTY's UK warehouse capacity during peak period
A+ content approval process — 3 rounds of edits required to meet Amazon brand guidelines
SOLUTIONS IMPLEMENTED

Strategic Responses

Created accelerated project timeline with weekly check-ins and hard deadlines for each workstream
Proactive image QA process — reviewed all assets against Amazon image requirements before ASIN submission
Video optimization workflow — established re-encoding protocol with COTY's creative team (1080p, H.264, <100MB)
Data-driven inventory negotiation — presented category benchmarks showing 10K units aligned with realistic 30-day sell-through
Shipment window buffer — built in 7-day window (Jan 18-25) to absorb potential warehouse delays
A+ content pre-approval review — internal QA against Amazon guidelines before formal submission, reducing approval cycles

06 · Strategic Learnings

Repeatable playbook for future NPD launches

What Worked

→ Early ASIN creation — 6 weeks pre-launch gave buffer for image/content revisions
→ Category benchmarking — Historical data prevented over-forecasting (rejected 15K, validated 10K)
→ 70/30 variant split — Aligned with mascara category norms (Black outperforms Brown 2:1)
→ Shipment window buffer — 7-day window absorbed warehouse capacity constraints
→ Pre-QA content workflow — Internal review before Amazon submission reduced approval cycles

Future Improvements

→ 10-week timeline — Add 2 weeks for brand video production to avoid re-encoding rush
→ Pre-launch sampling — Send units to Amazon Vine reviewers 2 weeks before launch for day-one reviews
→ Sponsored Brands campaign — Pre-schedule ads to activate on launch day (not post-launch)
→ Subscribe & Save eligibility — Enable from Day 1 to capture recurring purchase customers immediately
→ Post-launch reorder trigger — Set automated reorder point at 30% stock remaining (avoid stockout risk)
